Frequently Asked Questions

Questions most commonly asked, and their answers!

General

What is this site?

This is a living archive of my long-running creative projects, including worldbuilding, characters, lore systems, and related development notes.

Is everything here “canon”?

Not always. Some entries are works-in-progress, experimental drafts, or placeholders. If something is marked as “WIP,” treat it as subject to change.

Where should I start?

Start with the Vault hub, then choose Characters, Lore, or Systems. If you want a curated overview, check the Projects page.

Vault

What is the Vault?

The Vault is the primary archive index. It’s where I organize characters, lore, systems, realms, and other worldbuilding references into structured hubs.

Why are some vault pages empty or “Coming Soon”?

Some pages are scaffolding—placeholders that reserve structure while the content is being built. They’re intentionally present to keep navigation consistent.

How often is the Vault updated?

Updates happen in waves. Some areas may remain stable while others change rapidly during active development.

Projects

What counts as a “Main Project” vs “Side Project”?

Main Projects are long-running pillars with the most active development and structured documentation. Side Projects are experiments, prototypes, or smaller works that may evolve into something larger.

Do you have demos or playable builds?

If a demo exists, it’ll be linked from the relevant project page under “Explore” or “Demo.”

Can I follow development?

If I’m maintaining devlogs or updates, they’ll be linked from the project page or the Vault hub.

Usage & Permissions

Can I use your characters, lore, or setting?

By default: please don’t reuse major elements without permission. If you want to reference something, reach out and tell me what you’re planning.

Can I share links to your pages?

Yes—sharing links is generally fine. If something is marked private, sealed, or draft-only, please respect that.

Can I make fan work?

If you want to create fan art or inspired work, contact me first—especially if it’s public or monetized.

Contact

How can I contact you?

Use the contact methods listed on the homepage: Home → Contact.

What should I include in a message?

A short intro, what page/project you’re referencing, and what you’re trying to do. If it’s collaboration-related, include timeline + what you need from me.

Ask a Question

Send a question not already listed above, feedback, or a collaboration request.
